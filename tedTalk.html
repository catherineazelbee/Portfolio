Concept Art and Development

The second project in Animation Basics was a group project that had to be based around a TedTalk of our group's choice. Our group chose one about sleep, explaining the harms the modern person's sleep schedule has on their psyche, and how rethinking our sleep schedule can lend itself to an improved quality of life. The project has to be at least two minutes, and the portion of the talk we chose lent itself to this short time span. We did have to restructure some of the speech to fit this timeframe, leading to us editing the audio to create a clear structure for storytelling.

Something I wanted to focus on in this project was the animation to be consistent across all shots. I went ahead and designed a character turnaround sheet and animation, getting feedback from my teammates so I could finalize a design we would all be happy with for our main character. I was inspired by other TedAnimated character designs, wanting to create an easily animatable design with appealing, round shape language. Since we never planned on having our character viewed from behind, I only focused on front facing views for the sake of time.

Animatic Development

Our group of three decided to split the project so we would each work on a third of the entire project. We all worked on our alotted sections of the audio: Issy taking the first 41 seconds, myself taking the next 27 seconds, Fia taking the next 44 seconds, and myself taking the final 17 seconds. This division gave us all an equal amount of work of around 42 seconds each, with each of us being resposible for animatic, animation, and coloring of our assigned time frames.

While we were all able to complete our designated animatic sections, there were some setbacks in the completion of this project. Due to unexpected circumstances, our original division of roles had to reconsidered. Fia would not be able to work on the project for an extended period of time, so in order to have a completed project, I took it upon myself to do the character animation for these parts. Eventually, Fia was able to work again, doing the background animation for their original shots and the background painting for my ending shots.

In the end, I believe this project was a good practice in 2D Animation, focusing on the 12 Principles of Animation in my work. While I did have to do extra animation work, I think it helped me prepare for unexpected complications in group work, as well as how to find solutions to a problem without getting stuck. Despite these setbacks, I am proud of the project my group and I were able to create.

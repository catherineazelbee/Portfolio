Concept Art and Development

The second project in Animation Basics was a group project that had to be based around a TedTalk of our group's choice. Our group chose one about sleep, explaining the harms the modern person's sleep schedule has on their psyche, and how rethinking our sleep schedule can lend itself to an improved quality of life. The project has to be at least two minutes, and we felt as if the portion we chose lent itself to this short time span. We did have to restructure some of the speech to fit this timeframe and still have a clear story structure, leading to us editing the audio to create this clear structure.

Since we split the project so we would all be doing around a third of the shots in full, I wanted the animation to be consistent across all shots. I went ahead and designed this character turnaround sheet and animation, getting feedback from my teammated so I could finalize a design we would all be happy with. I was inspired by other TedAnimated character designs, wanting to create an easily animatable design with appealing shape language. Since we never planned on having our character viewed from behind, I only focused on front facing views.
